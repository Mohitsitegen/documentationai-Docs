## What you'll do

Take a Figma design and turn it into a real WordPress page. About 5 minutes start to finish.

## Before you start

Make sure you've completed:

- [Installed the plugin](/getting-started/installing-plugin) on your WordPress site
- [Connected the plugin](/getting-started/connect-plugin) to the SiteZen platform
- [Added your Figma token](/getting-started/figma-token) to the platform

If any of those are incomplete, the Convert button won't work. Finish them first.

## The Convert page tour

When you open the platform → **Convert**, you'll see fields for:

| Field | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **Figma section URL** | Tells SiteZen which Figma frame to read |
| **Figma access token** | Authorizes reading your Figma file |
| **Section screenshot** | Tells SiteZen exactly what the layout should look like |
| **Target WordPress site** | Where the converted result lands |
| **Where should this section land?** | Page, Template, or CPT |

We'll fill in each one.

## Step 1: Pick the right section in Figma

The best section to start with: **a simple hero section, an about block, or a CTA section** with mostly text + 1-2 images. Save complex tabs/sliders for later — start small.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your Figma file" icon="figma" title-type="p">
    Open Figma in a new tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the section frame" icon="search" title-type="p">
    In the **Layers panel** on the left, look for the top-level frame that represents your section. It should be the wrapper around all the content (heading + subhead + images + buttons), not an individual text node.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Right-click → Copy link to selection" icon="link" title-type="p">
    Right-click that frame → **Copy link to selection**. The URL is now in your clipboard.

    The URL looks like: `https://www.figma.com/design/abc123.../?node-id=12:345`
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
  **Pick the SECTION frame, not a child element.** If you right-click a single button or heading, SiteZen will only convert that one element. Right-click the OUTERMOST container of the section.
</Callout>

[SCREENSHOT: Figma layers panel with a section frame right-clicked, "Copy link to selection" highlighted]

## Step 2: Take a screenshot of the section

The screenshot is your ground-truth visual. SiteZen uses it to know exactly how the layout should look. Make it clean and accurate.

<Steps>
  <Step title="In Figma, zoom to fit the section" icon="zoom-in" title-type="p">
    Press **2** on your keyboard with the section selected — Figma zooms to fit.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Screenshot just the section" icon="image" title-type="p">
    - **Mac**: Cmd+Shift+4, drag a box around the section
    - **Windows**: Win+Shift+S, drag a box around the section
    - **Or**: in Figma, right-click the frame → **Copy as PNG**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify quality" icon="check" title-type="p">
    Open the screenshot in your file viewer. It should be sharp, show ALL the content in the section, and NOT include unrelated UI like Figma's panels.
  </Step>
</Steps>

[SCREENSHOT: Example of a good section screenshot — clean, all content visible, no Figma UI]

## Step 3: Fill in the Convert form

<Steps>
  <Step title="Paste the Figma URL" icon="clipboard" title-type="p">
    Paste the URL you copied in Step 1 into the **Figma section URL** field.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify the Figma token is there" icon="key" title-type="p">
    The **Figma access token** field should already show your token from when you set it up. If empty, paste it again from your Figma settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Drop in the screenshot" icon="upload" title-type="p">
    Drag your screenshot file onto the **Section screenshot** area, or click to browse. You'll see a thumbnail appear when it uploads.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick your target WordPress site" icon="globe" title-type="p">
    Select your site from the **Target WordPress site** dropdown. (If empty, you haven't connected a site yet — see [Connect Plugin](/getting-started/connect-plugin).)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose Page → New page" icon="file" title-type="p">
    Under **Where should this section land?**, click **Page**, then **New page**. This creates a fresh WordPress page for your conversion.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 4: Run the conversion

Click **Convert this section**. A progress indicator appears.

What happens during this ~30-60 seconds:
1. SiteZen reads your Figma file (text, colors, fonts, images)
2. Sends the data + your screenshot to the AI engine
3. AI generates HTML matching your design pixel-perfectly
4. Post-processing applies WordPress conventions
5. Preview loads in your browser

[SCREENSHOT: Loading state during conversion]

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
  First conversion takes longer (~45-60s) because the AI's cache is cold. Subsequent conversions in the same session are faster (~25-40s).
</Callout>

## Step 5: Review the preview

When conversion completes, you'll see your design rendered. Three viewports are available at the top:

- **Desktop** (default)
- **Tablet**
- **Mobile**

Click each to see how it responds. The mobile view will have stacked elements and shrunk fonts.

[SCREENSHOT: Preview page showing Desktop/Tablet/Mobile toggle]

### If it looks right → push to WordPress

Click **Push to WordPress** in the top right. After a few seconds, you'll get a success message with a link to the live page.

### If something's off → use Fix issues

Click **Fix issues** in the top right. A dialog appears where you can:
- Describe what's wrong in plain English ("the heading should be bigger", "the button color is wrong")
- Optionally upload a marked-up screenshot with arrows/circles pointing to the problem

Click **Apply fixes**. The AI re-runs with your feedback, making surgical fixes only to what you flagged.

See [Fix Issues retry flow](/advanced/fix-issues) for more.

## Step 6: View your live page

After pushing, click the page URL in the success notice (or go to WP Admin → Pages → find the new page → View).

You'll see your section live on WordPress. It's:
- **Fully editable** — click any element in the Gutenberg editor to change text, swap images, edit colors
- **Responsive** — mobile and tablet styles are already applied
- **Interactive** — tabs/accordions/sliders work without any extra setup

## Tips for great results

1. **Start with simple sections.** Hero, CTA, about — these convert nearly perfectly on the first try. Complex tabs/forms may need a retry.

2. **Use clean Figma files.** Auto-layout frames convert better than absolute-positioned chaos. If you have time, organize layers before converting.

3. **Match the screenshot to the section.** If your screenshot shows the whole page but the Figma URL points at just a sub-section (or vice versa), the engine gets confused.

4. **Verbatim text matters.** SiteZen copies your Figma text exactly. If you have placeholder lorem ipsum, it'll end up in WordPress. Replace it with real copy before converting.

5. **Use high-quality images in Figma.** SiteZen exports them at 2x resolution by default.

## Common issues

**"Conversion took too long (over 60s)"**
The section is too complex for the current plan's time limit. Either:
- Convert a smaller sub-section
- Upgrade to a plan with longer conversion time

**"Wrong section converted"**
The Figma URL points at a different element than your screenshot shows. Re-copy the link from the right frame (the outer section, not a child).

**"Result looks wrong"**
Use the [Fix Issues](/advanced/fix-issues) button to refine. The AI will only fix what you flag — won't change other parts.

**"Push to WordPress failed"**
Check that your WordPress site is reachable and the connection key is still valid. See [Connect Plugin troubleshooting](/getting-started/connect-plugin#common-issues).
